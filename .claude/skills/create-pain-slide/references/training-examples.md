# Training Examples

Three worked examples showing how raw input data is transformed into a structured
challenge table. Each example shows the source material and the expected output.

---

## Example 1: AIG

### Source material

**Company:** AIG

**Challenge area 1 - "AIG Next: creating a leaner parent company driving global consistency and local relevance across our end-to-end systems"**

- Issue: There are a lot of different tools across AIG, e.g. 200 different rating tools, leading to high costs and inconsistency throughout the organization.
- Impact:
  - Loss of revenue: AIG's expense ratio is higher than its competitors meaning it is less competitive on price.
  - Slower, more manual and error prone processes reducing productivity and slowing down transformational costs.
  - Management focus reduced by wider range of tech tools making it harder to be consistent and implement controls.
- Goal: $500m annual run rate savings to progress towards achieving 11-15% expense savings and adjusted ROCE in excess of 10%
- Owner: Roshan Navagamuwa, Global CIO
- Quote: "We can quote the same risk as Chubb but they will be able to beat us on price due to the expense load we have to put on each risk."

**Challenge area 2 - "AIG's board strategy is to achieve high-quality growth"**

- Issue: AIG cannot quote on all the submissions it receives and response times to brokers is delayed. Do not have the insights to move into new markets with confidence.
- Impact:
  - Missed revenue opportunities.
  - Lower than desired submission to quote ratio.
  - Lower than desired quote to bind ratio.
  - Unable to rollout raters at speed, act opportunistically and achieve growth in profitable lines, geographies and customer segments.
- Goal: Growth from $27.5bn to $33.25bn
- Owner: Peter Zaffino, CEO
- Quote: "Brokers need one thing they need is consistency and risk appetite."

**Challenge area 3 - "AIG's highest priority is underwriting excellence"**

- Issue: Lack of data/information at point of pricing to make informed decision, e.g. portfolio impact, CAT modelling. Challenging to analyse the entire portfolio to be able to identify rate/strategy changes.
- Impact:
  - Portfolio performance: inability to track portfolio performance will impact decision making and risk writing policies outside of portfolio guidelines.
  - Book volatility: Lack of accurate data may lead to underestimating risks, resulting in more frequent and severe claims than anticipated.
  - Rate adequacy: Uninformed decision making will not accurately reflect the underlying risk, leading to inconsistencies in pricing.
- Goal: Loss Ratio is 58.0% as of May 2024
- Owner: Chris Schaper, EVP Global Chief Underwriting Officer
- Quote: "Our unwavering commitment to underwriting excellence and ability to manage volatility remain fundamental to the sustainability of AIG's underwriting income growth."

### Expected output

| Challenge | Issue | Impact | Measure | Org Context | Objective |
|-----------|-------|--------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Fragmented technology estate | AIG operates with a highly fragmented tooling environment, including roughly 200 rating tools, creating inconsistency and a high operating cost base. | Higher expenses reduce pricing competitiveness, manual and error-prone workflows slow productivity and transformation, and fragmented systems make governance and control harder to enforce. | $500m annual run rate savings target<br>11-15% expense savings target<br>Adjusted ROCE >10%<br>Approx. 200 rating tools | Roshan Navagamuwa, Global CIO. Quote: AIG can quote the same risk as competitors but loses on price because of the expense load carried on each risk. | Consolidate and standardize tooling to reduce cost, improve consistency, and enable scalable operations. |
| Missed growth opportunities | AIG cannot quote on all submissions quickly enough and lacks the insight needed to pursue new markets confidently. | Delayed broker response times and incomplete quoting create lost revenue opportunities, reduce conversion through the funnel, and limit the business's ability to expand profitably. | Growth target: $27.5bn to $33.25bn<br>Submission-to-quote ratio<br>Quote-to-bind ratio | Peter Zaffino, CEO. Quote: Brokers need consistency and clarity on risk appetite. | Increase quote throughput and responsiveness to capture more opportunities and drive profitable growth. |

## Example 2: Ascot Group

### Source material

**Company:** Ascot Group

**Challenge area 1 - "Ascot does not have the data and insights needed to inform its pricing and overall strategy"**

- Issue: Underwriters are unable to pull in information when pricing a risk; data takes 1.5 months to be uploaded.
- Impact:
  - Poor risk selection: $40m loss in property.
  - Error in rekeying: $100k loss.
  - c.$4m missed premium.
- Goal: 2% loss ratio improvement ($11.9m)
- Owner: Jessie Paulson, CUO US
- Quote: "2 hours+ to enter claims data when pricing a renewal."

### Expected output

| Challenge | Issue | Impact | Measure | Org Context | Objective |
|-----------|-------|--------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Lack of pricing insight | Underwriters cannot access complete claims and risk information in real time, with data lags reaching 1.5 months. | Incomplete visibility leads to poor risk selection and manual rekeying errors, resulting in avoidable losses and missed premium. | $40m property loss exposure<br>$100k rekeying error<br>1.5 month data lag<br>2+ hours data entry | Jessie Paulson, CUO US. Quote: Underwriters use only a sample of claims history because entry takes 2+ hours. | Enable real-time integrated access to claims data to improve pricing accuracy. |

## Example 3: Bupa

### Source material

**Company:** Bupa

**Challenge area 1 - "Manual, Fragmented Pricing Tools"**

- Issue: Pricing relies on complex, locally-maintained Excel spreadsheets with many versions leading to duplication.
- Impact:
  - Unscalable processes block collaboration.
  - Vulnerability to loss of knowledge if key people leave.
- Goal: Centralise pricing in a single platform.
- Owner: Peter Bradley, Group Commercial Actuarial Director
- Quote: "There's 20 versions of that [spreadsheet] all around the world."

**Challenge area 2 - "Inefficient and Risk-Prone Operations"**

- Issue: Heavy manual work for batch pricing and renewals causes bottlenecks.
- Impact:
  - Batch rating involves running macros across hundreds of documents.
  - Business exposed to disruption if processes break.
- Goal: Automate batch pricing and renewals.
- Owner: Peter Bradley
- Quote: "In April, there's 400 documents... it takes 30 seconds [per] document."

### Expected output

| Challenge | Issue | Impact | Measure | Org Context | Objective |
|-----------|-------|--------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Fragmented pricing tools | Global pricing operations rely on inconsistent, locally-maintained spreadsheets, creating version-control risks and duplication. | Unscalable processes hinder global collaboration and create significant key-person dependency risks. | 20+ versions of core spreadsheets globally<br>High risk of version-control failure | Peter Bradley, Group Commercial Actuarial Director. Quote: There are 20 versions of the same spreadsheet globally. | Centralize pricing onto a single platform to ensure global consistency and resilience. |
| Operational bottlenecks | Manual batch pricing and renewal workflows create significant operational drag and exposure to disruption. | Actuarial capacity is wasted on slow, repetitive tasks, delaying rate reviews and increasing error risk. | 400+ documents processed manually in April<br>30 seconds per document macro execution | Peter Bradley, Group Commercial Actuarial Director. Quote: Batch rating 400 documents is a long, 'painful' process. | Automate batch pricing to improve operational speed and auditability. |
