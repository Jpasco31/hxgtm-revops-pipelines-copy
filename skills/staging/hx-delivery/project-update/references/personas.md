# Persona Definitions

Each persona has different priorities when receiving a project update. Tailor the content, depth, and framing accordingly.

## Project Manager

**Role context**: Owns day-to-day delivery. Tracks tasks, timelines, dependencies, and resource allocation. Accountable for keeping the project on schedule and within scope.

**Priorities**: Task-level progress, blockers requiring action, timeline adherence, upcoming milestones, resource constraints, dependency chains.

**Update structure**:

1. **Progress snapshot** — Percentage or ratio of tasks completed vs total. Break down by phase or workstream if available.
2. **Milestone tracker** — Which milestones are on track, at risk, or overdue. Include dates.
3. **Blockers and actions** — Each blocker with owner, age, and suggested resolution path.
4. **Upcoming work** — Tasks due in the next 1-2 weeks with assignees.
5. **Risks** — Delivery risks with likelihood and impact assessment.
6. **Dependencies** — Cross-team or external dependencies and their status.

**Tone**: Detailed and operational. Use specific numbers, dates, and task names. Flag anything that needs PM intervention.

## Customer Success Manager

**Role context**: Owns the customer relationship. Focused on customer satisfaction, adoption, and long-term account health. Acts as the customer's internal advocate.

**Priorities**: Customer sentiment, engagement quality, adoption milestones, relationship risks, handoff readiness, opportunities to strengthen the partnership.

**Update structure**:

1. **Relationship health** — Overall assessment of where the customer relationship stands based on project momentum.
2. **Engagement summary** — Key interactions, decisions made, and customer responsiveness.
3. **Adoption indicators** — Progress toward go-live or value realisation milestones.
4. **Risks to the relationship** — Delays, unmet expectations, or communication gaps that could erode trust.
5. **Opportunities** — Moments to deepen engagement, showcase value, or identify expansion potential.
6. **Handoff readiness** — For onboarding projects, how close the customer is to self-sufficiency.

**Tone**: Relationship-oriented. Frame progress through the lens of customer experience and satisfaction. Highlight what matters for retention and growth.

## Sales Executive

**Role context**: Owns the commercial relationship. Interested in deal progression, revenue impact, and account expansion. Often less involved in day-to-day delivery.

**Priorities**: Commercial implications, renewal/expansion signals, executive-level status, red flags that could affect the deal, competitive positioning.

**Update structure**:

1. **Executive summary** — 2-3 sentence overview of project health and trajectory.
2. **Commercial signals** — Anything indicating expansion appetite, budget conversations, or competitive threats.
3. **Customer satisfaction** — High-level read on how the customer perceives the engagement.
4. **Red flags** — Issues that could jeopardise renewal or expansion. Keep brief but specific.
5. **Opportunities** — Upsell or cross-sell openings surfaced by the project work.
6. **Recommended actions** — Specific next steps for the Sales Executive (e.g., "Schedule exec check-in" or "Reference this success in QBR").

**Tone**: Concise and commercially oriented. Lead with what matters for revenue. Avoid delivery minutiae — surface only what affects the deal.

---

## Executive Sponsor

**Role context**: Senior leader with strategic oversight. Sponsors multiple initiatives. Needs to understand portfolio health and make resource allocation decisions.

**Priorities**: Strategic alignment, overall health, escalations requiring executive action, cross-project patterns, high-level risks and opportunities.

**Update structure**:

1. **Status indicator** — One-line health assessment (On Track / At Risk / Off Track) with brief justification.
2. **Strategic summary** — 2-3 sentences on what this project means for the broader account or portfolio.
3. **Key achievements** — Top 2-3 accomplishments since last update.
4. **Escalations** — Items that need executive attention or decision-making. Be specific about what action is needed.
5. **Strategic risks** — Risks with business-level impact (e.g., timeline threatening a renewal date, scope changes affecting ROI).
6. **Outlook** — Forward-looking statement on trajectory and next major milestone.

**Tone**: Strategic and concise. No task-level detail. Every point should connect to business outcomes. Respect their limited time — be brief and decisive.
